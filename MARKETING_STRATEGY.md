## üõ°Ô∏è Addressing Objections

### "Why would I lock up my trading capital?"

**Answer**: 
You're not locking it up‚Äîyou can withdraw anytime. SAS is YOUR vault with YOUR keys. The "lock" is psychological, not technical. And that's the point: it prevents you from rage-trading your savings away.

**Messaging**: "It's not locked. It's safe. There's a difference."

---

### "I can just save manually"

**Answer**: 
Sure, and you can also manually eat healthy, manually go to the gym, and manually not check your phone at 2am. But do you? No. Automation removes willpower from the equation.

**Messaging**: "You won't. That's the problem. SAS does it for you."

---

### "0.4% fee is too high"

**Answer**: 
- You lose more than 0.4% on EVERY bad trade
- You spend 0.4% on network fees anyway
- This 0.4% goes to YOUR FUTURE, not to some random validator
- Trading platforms charge 0.05-0.3% per swap already‚Äîthis is incremental and goes to savings

**Messaging**: "You'll waste 10% on a rug pull, but complain about 0.4% that saves you? Do the math."

---

### "I don't trust smart contracts"

**Answer**: 
- Non-custodial: YOU control your PDA vault
- Open-source: Audit the code yourself on GitHub
- Battle-tested: Built with Anchor, Solana's most trusted framework
- No admin keys: Protocol can't rug you

**Messaging**: "It's trustless. That's the whole point of crypto."

---

### "Why Solana and not Ethereum?"

**Answer**: 
- Speed: Auto-savings happen in the SAME transaction as your trade (impossible on ETH)
- Cost: Fees are ~$0.00001 on Solana vs. $5-50 on Ethereum
- UX: Seamless integration without gas wars

**Messaging**: "Because Solana is built for this. Ethereum isn't."
